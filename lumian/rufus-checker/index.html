Our approach

Score what Rufus weighs.

Rufus relies on structured catalog data, A+ content, customer Q&A, and reviews. We scrape those signals, score them with documented heuristics, and surface fixes ranked by recoverable points.

The scoring rubric

7 layers, 100 points.

Weights derived from ALFI's framework + Amalytix 1,300-product Rufus pattern study. Catalog architecture is the heaviest because Rufus retrieves on structured backend data, not consumer copy.

What we learned

Three signals worth selling.

A+
A+ content drives biggest delta.
Switch (with A+) scored 56 vs Echo Dot (no A+) at 33. Single biggest controllable lever.
Q&A·
Q&A is universally absent.
All 6 ASINs scored 0/20 because the actor doesn't expose Q&A. Real bottleneck. Solving next.
25%
Catalog architecture pays off.
Filling backend attributes is the heaviest weighted layer, and the most-skipped by sellers.

Insight that sells: no Apple, Amazon, or Nintendo listing tested above the Rufus-Ready threshold. Even billion-dollar brands have huge optimization room.
